# Goal outcomes outrank plan details

Goal objective and achievement criteria are authoritative over referenced
requirements, which in turn outrank the accepted plan's implementation details.
Planning and review therefore emphasize observable outcomes and evidence rather
than prescribing mechanics; reasonable plan divergence is acceptable when the
Goal remains satisfied, while completing every plan step is insufficient when
the Goal is not achieved. Achievement criteria remain within the Goal's
free-form completion contract and are restated as observable evidence in plans;
no separate persisted criteria field is introduced.
